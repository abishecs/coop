Pure Industrial

This work term marked my introduction to the tech workforce, and it initially challenged many of my preconceived notions. I entered with the impression that the tech industry demanded a strictly serious and professional demeanor at all times. However, my first day at Pure Industrial quickly cleared up this misconception.

The day began with a challenge: navigating to the office. Having grown up in Toronto during my early childhood, I assumed familiarity with the city, but returning after more than a decade proved otherwise. After a lengthy 2.5-hour search, I finally arrived at the office, where my initial frustrations were soon replaced with warmth and excitement. I was greeted with a welcome page and a thoughtful assortment of materials to help me settle in.

The onboarding process was seamless and well-organized. Upon receiving my laptop, I was guided by an IT team member through the setup process. I was also introduced to a series of training videos covering company software, workflows, behavioral expectations, and essential skills. Lori Hipwell, who oversaw my time at Pure Industrial, personally took the time to introduce me to each of my colleagues across various departments, regardless of whether I would work closely with them. From the Data Analysis and Sustainability teams to Katerina at the front desk, who greeted and bid farewell to everyone with a smile; this welcoming environment transformed my perspective on the tech industry within hours.
